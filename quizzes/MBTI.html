The Myers-Briggs Type Indicator (MBTI) is an introspective personality test designed to identify a person's personality type, strengths, and preferences. Instead of looking at "good" or "bad" traits, it explores how you naturally process information and interact with the world.

🧩 5 Fascinating Facts About MBTI

1. Created by a Mother-Daughter Duo

Unlike many psychological tests created by professors in labs, the MBTI was developed by Katharine Cook Briggs and her daughter Isabel Briggs Myers. They weren't psychologists—they were brilliant observers of human behavior.

2. Based on Carl Jung’s "Cognitive Functions"

The test is built on the theories of the famous Swiss psychiatrist Carl Jung. He believed that humans use four main mental functions: Sensing, Intuition, Thinking, and Feeling.

3. The "Golden" 16 Types

The test sorts everyone into 16 unique types using four scales:

  • E vs. I: Where do you get your energy? (Extroversion/Introversion)
  • S vs. N: How do you take in information? (Sensing/Intuition)
  • T vs. F: How do you make decisions? (Thinking/Feeling)
  • J vs. P: How do you organize your life? (Judging/Perceiving)

4. The Rarest Type: INFJ

While statistics vary, the INFJ is consistently ranked as the rarest personality type, making up only about 1-2% of the global population.

5. It’s About Preference, Not Ability

A great way to explain MBTI is the "Handwriting Metaphor." You can write with both hands, but one feels natural and effortless. MBTI shows you which "mental hand" is your dominant one.
